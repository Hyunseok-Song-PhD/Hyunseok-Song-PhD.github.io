About

I am an Assistant Professor of Sports Administration at Northern State University. My research bridges traditional sport marketing and consumer behavior with emerging digital contexts—including online sports betting, esports, and AI integration—underpinned by rigorous quantitative methodologies. I earned my Ph.D. in Sport Management from Indiana University, Bloomington, where my dissertation focused on identifying online sports betting motivations associated with betting intention.

Research Interests

Traditional Marketing & Sport Consumer Behavior

Examining service evaluations, service failures, and cross-cultural spectating behaviors (e.g., post-global health crisis perspectives) within the sport industry.

Emerging Digital Sport Markets

Investigating psychological motivations and consumption patterns in high-growth sectors—specifically online sports betting, esports, and the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI).

Sport Analytics & Quantitative Methodology

Applying advanced data analytics to understand complex consumer dynamics and advancing methodological rigor (e.g., publication bias, predictive modeling) in sport management scholarship.

Teaching

My pedagogical approach is built upon practical application and data-driven decision-making in sport management, spanning three core pillars:

Sport Business & Marketing Management

Strategic Management, Financial Aspects of Sports, Event & Facility Management, Sport Marketing, Sport Fan Behavior, eSports Management, Digital Currency, International Sport, Sport Law & Ethics.

Research Methodology & Academic Dissemination

Research Methods, Research & Assessment in Sport. Mentoring students in scholarly dissemination and bridging classroom learning with professional research presentation.

Advanced Analytics & Data Science

Sport Analytics, Big Data & Statistical Analysis in Sport, Performance-Based Data Analytics, Applied Network Analysis. Applying complex quantitative methods to solve real-world sport business challenges.
